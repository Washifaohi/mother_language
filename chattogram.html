Language of Chattogram

Chattogram is one of the largest and most important cities in southeastern Bangladesh.

The city is a major seaport and hub for trade, business, and industry.

The primary language is Bengali (Bangla), but the local Chattogram dialect (Chittagonian) is widely spoken and culturally distinctive.

The Chattogram dialect differs from standard Bangla in pronunciation, vocabulary, and tone.

Influenced by the hills, sea, and trade, it has a unique rhythm and fast, expressive speech.

Features of Chattogram Dialect

  • Fast and energetic pronunciation with a unique rhythm.
  • Use of many distinct words not found in standard Bangla.
  • Different pronunciation of common Bangla sounds.
  • Influence from neighboring regions and trade.
  • Shortened and modified sentence structures.
  • Expressive, lively, and culturally rich style.

Importance of Mother Language

People of Chattogram honor 21 February as International Mother Language Day.

They visit Shaheed Minar, place flowers, walk barefoot, and organize cultural events to celebrate Bangla.

Example of Local Speech

1. Standard Bangla: "তুমি কোথায় যাচ্ছ?"
Chattogram Dialect: "তুঁই গইয়্যা যান?"

2. Standard Bangla: "তুমি কি করছ?"
Chattogram Dialect: "তুঁই কি গর?"

3. Standard Bangla: "আমি বাড়ি যাচ্ছি।"
Chattogram Dialect: "আই বাড়ি যান।"

4. Standard Bangla: "তুমি খেয়েছ?"
Chattogram Dialect: "তুঁই খাইছ?"

5. Standard Bangla: "কোথায় যাবে?"
Chattogram Dialect: "কই যাইবা?"

Date: 21 February
Declared by: UNESCO as International Mother Language Day
First observed: 2000
A day to honor the sacrifices of language martyrs and celebrate linguistic diversity.

Significance: This day reminds us of the importance of preserving our mother languages and promoting education, unity, and cultural heritage across the world.
