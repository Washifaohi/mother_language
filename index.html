International Mother Language Day

21 February

International Mother Language Day is observed every year on 21 February to celebrate and protect the diversity of languages spoken across the world.

The day highlights the importance of mother languages in preserving cultural identity, history, and traditions.

It promotes linguistic and cultural diversity by encouraging people to respect, learn, and use their native languages alongside other languages.

International Mother Language Day also supports multilingual education, which helps improve communication, understanding, and unity among different communities.

By raising awareness about endangered languages, this day reminds us that every language is valuable and plays a vital role in shaping human civilization. Learn more...

History of International Mother Language Day

International Mother Language Day is observed on 21 February to honor the historic language movement of 1952 in Bangladesh.

On this day, students sacrificed their lives to protect their mother tongue, Bangla, after Urdu was declared the sole national language of Pakistan.

The movement led to the recognition of Bangla as a national language in 1956 and later as the official language of independent Bangladesh in 1971.

In 1999, UNESCO proclaimed 21 February as International Mother Language Day to promote linguistic diversity, multilingualism, and the protection of all mother languages around the world.

Importance of the Day

This day holds great significance as it celebrates and safeguards the richness of our mother languages. Observing it helps raise awareness about the value of linguistic diversity and the importance of preserving cultural heritage.

  • Protects and preserves mother languages
  • Promotes cultural diversity and understanding
  • Encourages multilingual education and learning
  • Pays tribute to the brave language martyrs

International Mother Language Day in Bangladesh

Every year, countless people gather at the Shaheed Minar, removing their shoes and standing barefoot on the sacred ground as a symbol of reverence. With hearts full of gratitude and heads bowed in solemn respect, they place colorful flowers at the monument’s base, honoring the brave language martyrs who sacrificed their lives to protect and celebrate the mother tongue. The air is filled with quiet reflection, a poignant reminder of the courage and unity that preserved the identity and dignity of a people through their language.

Youth & Digital Advocacy (2026 Focus)

In 2026, International Mother Language Day places a special focus on the role of youth and digital innovation in preserving languages. Young creators, students, and activists are using technology to protect and promote their mother tongues in creative and impactful ways.

Digital platforms such as mobile apps, online dictionaries, social media, and virtual archives are helping document endangered languages and make them accessible to future generations. Through videos, blogs, and educational content, young people are raising awareness about linguistic diversity across the globe.

Language learning apps, podcasts, and digital storytelling are also making it easier for people to learn and use their native languages alongside global languages. These efforts ensure that no language is forgotten and that every culture continues to thrive in the digital age.

By combining tradition with technology, youth are becoming powerful advocates for preserving language heritage and building a more inclusive, multilingual world.

  • Encourages youth participation in language preservation
  • Uses digital tools to document endangered languages
  • Promotes language learning through apps and online platforms
  • Supports digital archives and cultural storytelling
  • Builds global awareness through social media
