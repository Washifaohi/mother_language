Language of Khulna

Khulna is one of the major and most important cities in southwestern Bangladesh. The city is renowned for its rich cultural heritage, historical significance, rivers, and close proximity to the Sundarbans, the largest mangrove forest in the world.

The primary language spoken in Khulna is Bengali (Bangla), which is also the national language of Bangladesh. Almost all residents use Bangla for daily communication, education, business, and cultural activities. In addition to standard Bangla, many people in Khulna speak a local dialect that reflects the traditions, customs, and lifestyle of the region.

Features of Khulna Dialect

  • Clear, soft, and smooth pronunciation that sounds calm and natural.
  • Use of unique local vocabulary words that may differ slightly from standard Bangla.
  • Strong influence from southern coastal speech patterns due to geographical location.
  • Friendly, warm, and expressive tone in daily conversation.
  • Shortened verb forms and informal expressions in casual speech.
  • Simple and direct sentence structure in everyday communication.
  • Emotional and respectful way of speaking, especially with elders.

Importance of Mother Language

Like the rest of Bangladesh, the people of Khulna deeply respect and honor 21 February as International Mother Language Day. They remember the brave language martyrs who sacrificed their lives in 1952 to protect and establish Bangla as a state language. People visit Shaheed Minar, offer flowers, walk barefoot in remembrance, and organize cultural programs, discussions, and songs celebrating the beauty and strength of the mother language.

Example of Local Speech

1. Standard Bangla: "তুমি কোথায় যাচ্ছ?"
Khulna Dialect: "তুই কই যাস?"

2. Standard Bangla: "তুমি কি করছ?"
Khulna Dialect: "তুই কী করস?"

3. Standard Bangla: "আমি এখন বাড়ি যাচ্ছি।"
Khulna Dialect: "আমি অহন বাড়ি যাইতাছি।"

4. Standard Bangla: "খাবার খেয়েছ?"
Khulna Dialect: "ভাত খাইছস?"

5. Standard Bangla: "তুমি আমাকে বলো।"
Khulna Dialect: "তুই আমারে ক।"

6. Standard Bangla: "সে কোথায় গেছে?"
Khulna Dialect: "ও কই গ্যাছে?"

7. Standard Bangla: "আমরা বাজারে যাব।"
Khulna Dialect: "আমরা বাজারে যামু।"

Date: 21 February
Declared by: UNESCO as International Mother Language Day
First observed: 2000
A day to honor the sacrifices of language martyrs and celebrate linguistic diversity.

Significance: This day reminds us of the importance of preserving our mother languages and promoting education, unity, and cultural heritage across the world.
