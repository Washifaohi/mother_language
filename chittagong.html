Languages of Chittagong Hill Tracts (CHT)

The Chittagong Hill Tracts, located in southeastern Bangladesh, is a culturally rich and ethnically diverse region. It is home to several indigenous communities, each with its own unique language and traditions.

Major communities include the Chakma, Marma, S’gaw Karen (Sawthal), Garo, Rakhine, and others. These languages are distinct from standard Bangla in pronunciation, grammar, and vocabulary, reflecting centuries of cultural heritage.

While Bangla is widely understood for official purposes, education, and trade, the local languages preserve the identity and traditions of each community.

These languages have unique phonetics, tonal patterns, and sentence structures. Many words are entirely different from Bangla, and expressions often carry local cultural meanings. Speaking in these languages conveys respect, friendliness, and the rich heritage of the people of the CHT.

Features of CHT Languages

  • Distinct phonetic systems and tonal variations.
  • Rich vocabulary reflecting local culture, nature, and traditions.
  • Sentence structures vary from standard Bangla.
  • Influence from neighboring regions, Myanmar, and historical trade languages.
  • Expressive, melodic, and culturally symbolic speech.
  • Languages are often oral, with traditional storytelling, songs, and rituals.
  • Community-specific accents and expressions make each language unique.
  • Friendly, respectful, and community-oriented communication style.

Importance of Mother Languages

Like all regions of Bangladesh, the people of the Chittagong Hill Tracts honor and celebrate 21 February as International Mother Language Day.

This day reminds everyone of the importance of preserving mother languages, cultural identity, and traditions.

Communities organize cultural programs, storytelling sessions, songs, and dances to celebrate the diversity of languages and promote intergenerational knowledge.

Respecting and protecting these languages ensures that future generations can continue to cherish their heritage.

Example of Local Speech (Bangla)

1. Standard Bangla: "তুমি কেমন আছো?"
Chakma: "নাংসে কোহ?"
Marma: "নাং লে হম?"
S'gaw Karen (Sawthal): "নাও কাও?"
Garo: "আমি নক?"
Rakhine: "না কাং লা?"

2. Standard Bangla: "আমি বাড়ি যাচ্ছি।"
Chakma: "আমি বাড়ি জা?"
Marma: "কা বাই জা?"
S'gaw Karen (Sawthal): "নাও লার আ?"
Garo: "আঙ্গা ওকনা জা?"
Rakhine: "ংগাই থাউ জা?"

3. Standard Bangla: "তুমি খেয়েছ?"
Chakma: "নাং খাইছ?"
Marma: "নাং হম খাইছ?"
S'gaw Karen (Sawthal): "နာ ဟာ ခို?"
Garo: "না খা?"
Rakhine: "না খাইছ?"

4. Standard Bangla: "কোথায় যাবে?"
Chakma: "কোই জাইবা?"
Marma: "কই জাইবা?"
S'gaw Karen (Sawthal): "নাও লা?"
Garo: "কোই যাম?"
Rakhine: "কোই যাইবা?"

5. Standard Bangla: "আমরা যাচ্ছি।"
Chakma: "আমরা জাচ্ছি।"
Marma: "আমরা জাচ্ছি।"
S'gaw Karen (Sawthal): "নাও লার আ।"
Garo: "আমরা জাচ্ছি।"
Rakhine: "আমরা যাইতাছি।"

Date: 21 February
Declared by: UNESCO as International Mother Language Day
First observed: 2000
A day to honor the sacrifices of language martyrs and celebrate linguistic diversity.

Significance: This day reminds us of the importance of preserving our mother languages and promoting education, unity, and cultural heritage across the world.
