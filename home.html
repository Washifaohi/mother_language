International Mother Language Day

21 February

International Mother Language Day is celebrated each year on 21 February to honor and safeguard the rich diversity of languages spoken around the globe.

This day emphasizes the vital role mother languages play in preserving cultural identity, history, and traditions.

It encourages respect for linguistic and cultural diversity by inspiring people to value, learn, and use their native languages alongside others.

The day also promotes multilingual education, which strengthens communication, mutual understanding, and unity among different communities.

By drawing attention to endangered languages, International Mother Language Day reminds us that every language matters and contributes uniquely to human civilization. Learn more...

History of International Mother Language Day

International Mother Language Day is observed on 21 February to commemorate the historic language movement of 1952 in Bangladesh.

On this day, students gave their lives to defend their mother tongue, Bangla, after Urdu was declared the only national language of Pakistan.

The movement resulted in Bangla being recognized as a national language in 1956 and later becoming the official language of independent Bangladesh in 1971.

In 1999, UNESCO declared 21 February as International Mother Language Day to promote linguistic diversity, multilingualism, and the protection of mother languages worldwide.

Importance of the Day

This day holds deep significance as it celebrates and protects the richness of our mother languages. Observing it helps spread awareness about the value of linguistic diversity and the importance of preserving cultural heritage.

  • Preserves and protects mother languages
  • Promotes cultural diversity and mutual understanding
  • Encourages multilingual education and lifelong learning
  • Honors the courage and sacrifice of the language martyrs

Did You Know?

  • More than 7,000 languages are spoken across the world today.
  • Nearly half of the world's languages are at risk of extinction.
  • Alongside Bangla, Bangladesh is home to over 40 indigenous languages.
  • UNESCO actively promotes multilingual education to protect cultural diversity.

International Mother Language Day in Bangladesh

Each year, thousands gather at the Shaheed Minar, removing their shoes and standing barefoot on the sacred ground as a mark of deep respect. With bowed heads, they lay vibrant flowers to honor the brave language martyrs, reflecting the courage, unity, and sacrifice that protected a nation’s identity.

Multilingual Education & Heritage

Multilingual education plays a vital role in preserving cultural heritage and promoting inclusive learning. It encourages people to learn in their mother tongue while also gaining knowledge of other languages, creating a balanced and enriched educational experience.

Learning through one's mother language helps improve understanding, confidence, and academic success, especially for young learners. It allows students to connect deeply with their culture, traditions, and identity while building a strong foundation for lifelong learning.

Around the world, colorful and interactive digital maps are being used to illustrate linguistic diversity. These maps showcase thousands of languages, helping people explore different cultures and understand the importance of preserving endangered languages.

By combining modern technology with traditional knowledge, multilingual education ensures that cultural heritage is protected and passed on to future generations.

  • Promotes learning through mother tongue
  • Improves understanding and academic performance
  • Preserves cultural identity and traditions
  • Highlights global linguistic diversity through digital maps
  • Supports endangered language preservation
